The goal was to create a free-to-play mobile game prototype in one month, in a team of 8: 4 gameplay programmers and 4 game designers.

It was my first time working with game design students, and this collaboration taught me a lot.

The game is a Vampire Survivors-like, with a twist: the player doesn’t control their movement, instead, they are pushed around by the recoil of their weapons.

I managed to accomplish a lot of tasks during this project:

- Implementation of a fully custom physics system using a grid, object pooling, and batch processing to optimize performance as much as possible

- Implementation of different enemy behaviors

- Extensive QA testing and bug fixing

- A strong focus on game feel (“juiciness”), taking inspiration from games such as Vampire Survivors, Holocure, Megabonk, Survivor.io, Diep.io, and even the temporary Swarm mode in League of Legends

- Work on a couple VFX, including the laser and stomp weapon previews and their associated visual effects
